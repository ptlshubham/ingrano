Organic Products

Organic food is grown without the use of synthetic chemicals, such as human-made pesticides and fertilizers, and does not contain genetically modified organisms (GMOs).

Organic agriculture is generally beneficial to the environment. Using organic production methods eliminates the use of synthetic fertilizers and pesticides, reducing chemical runoff and pollution of soils and waterways. Due to the use of on-farm inputs, such as fertilizers derived from compost produced on-site, smaller-scale organic farming often provides significant environmental benefits. Comparatively, large-scale organic farms often require inputs generated off-site and may not use integrated farming practices. Rather than increasing soil fertility within the farm, these operations may purchase specific allowable inputs, such as fish emulsion or blood meal to use as fertilizers. In spite of the fact that industrial agriculture uses fewer synthetic chemicals, off-farm inputs usually use more fossil fuels than on-farm inputs, which may not promote long-term sustainability.

Organic Pulses

Pulses are an important protein source in countries like ours. Besides having twice the protein content of most cereals, even our everyday dals have fiber, vitamins, and minerals if they are organically sourced. By increasing soil nitrogen and organic matter, Ingrano organic pulses promote sustainable agriculture in addition to being healthy, nutritious, and easy to cook with.
